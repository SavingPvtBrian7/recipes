Oriental Chicken Salad

1 head lettuce, shredded
6 green onions, chopped
1 c. cooked, diced chicken
Dressing:
2 tbsp. sugar
1/4 c. rice vinegar
1/4 c. salad oil
1/2 to 1 tbsp. toasted sesame oil
1 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. pepper
Chow mein noodles

Combine lettuce and green onions; chill. Toast sesame seeds and almonds in
butter (about 1 tbsp) in a small stove top pan. Combine with chilled salad.
Toss dressing with salad, then add a handful of dry chow mein noodles.
Dressing: Mix together sugar, rice vinegar, salad oil, toasted sesame oil,
salt and pepper.

Donna Kramer

Clam Chowder

4 Potatoes (unpeeled) diced small
1 medium onion, chopped fine
1/4 c. cut up bacon
2 cans minced clams (reserve liquid)
2 c. milk
1 c. light cream
Dash of pepper

potatoes in pot. Add onion and bacon, then clams and clam juice. Add milk and

Terrific Three-Bean Soup

2 (16 oz.) cans tomatoes, cut up
1 (15 1/2 oz.) can red kidney beans
1 (15 oz.) can Great Northern beans
1 (15 oz.) can garbanzo beans
3 medium onions, chopped (1 1/2 c.)
2 medium green peppers, chopped
2 stalks celery, sliced (2 c.)
1 medium yellow summer squash, sliced
1/2 c. water
2 tsp. chili powder

add salt to taste. Makes 10 servings.

Beef Parmesan

2 lb. round steak
2 eggs, beaten
1/2 c. Parmesan cheese
3/4 c. Italian bread crumbs
10 slices Mozzarella cheese
1 medium onion, chopped
1 c. hot water
1 (6 oz.) can tomato paste
1/3 c. cooking oil
1 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. pepper
1/2 mayonnaise
1/2 tsp. garlic
1/2 tsp. Worcestershire sauce
1/2 c. grated Mozzarella cheese

Slice steak thin; trim off fat. Dip into egg; roll in mixture of Parmesan and
bread crumbs. Heat oil. Brown steak on both sides. Place 1/4 of meat in
greased casserole. Layer 1/4 of Mozzarella cheese; repeat until gone.
Brown onion. Add water, tomato paste and seasonings. Boil for 5 minutes. Pour
over steak. Top with gratd cheese. Bake for 40 to 60 minutes at 350 degrees.
Serve over noodles.

Susan Armstrong

Easy Homemade Pizza

Sauce:
1 (8 oz.) can tomato sauce
2 tsp. oregano
1/2 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. garlic powder
1/4 tsp. pepper
Dough:
1 pkg. active dry yeast
1 c. warm water
2 1/2 c. all-purpose flour
2 tbsp. vegetable oil
1 tsp. sugar
1 tsp. salt
Additional:
3 c. Mozzarella, shredded
1/4 c. Parmesan or Romano cheese, grated

Dough: Preheat oven to 425 degrees. Dissolve yeast in warm water (105 to 115
degrees). Stir in remaining ingredients. Beat vigorously 20 strokes. Let rest
for 5 minutes. Mix tomato sauce with spices. Divide dough into halves. Roll
out onto greased cookie sheets.
Spread tomato sauce mixture and sprinkle with cheeses. Bake on lowest rack for
15 to 20 minutes.

Cinnamon Bread

6 c. sifted flour
1 tbsp. baking powder
1 1/2 tsp. baking soda
1 1/2 tsp. salt
3/4 c. unsalted butter or margarine
3 c. sugar
6 large eggs
3 c. buttermilk
1 tbsp. vanilla
Streusel-Combine:
6 tbsp. sugar
1 tbsp. cinnamon

Sift flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Cream butter and 3 cups
sugar; add eggs. Beat flour mixture alternately with buttermilk into butter 
mixture, beginning and ending with flour mix. Stir in vanilla. Pour half
batter into 2 large greased Bundt pans. Sprinkle 3/4 of cinnamon/sugar mix
over batter. Add remaining batter and sprinkle top with remaining sugar. Bake
at 350 degrees until wooden toothpick comes out clean, about 50 to 60 minutes.
Cool completely in pan on wire track

Low Salt, Low Fat Banana Bread

1/2 c. margarine, softened
3/4 c. brown sugar
1 egg
1 c. whole wheat flour
1/2 c. white flour
1 tsp. baking soda
1/2 tsp. cinnamon
3 ripe bananas, mashed
1/4 c. nonfat plain yogurt
1 c. chopped walnuts

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Cream margarine and sugar until smooth, then blend in the egg. Mix the bananas and yogurt. Set aside. In a bowl, sift flours, baking soda and cinnamon. To the margarine mixture, alternately add portions of the banana mixture and the flours, mixing well after each addition. Stir in the walnuts.
Grease a 9x5 inch loaf pan. Pour the mixture into the pan. Bake for 45 to an hour. After you remove the bread from the oven, let it stand for 15 minutes. Remove it from the pan and let it stand for 1 hour. Makes 1 loaf or 12 muffins.

Anonymous

Halloween Cookies

2 c. flour
1 c. oats
1 tsp. baking soda
1 tsp. cinnamon
1/2 tsp. salt
1 c. butter or margarine, softened
1 c. packed brown sugar
1 c. sugar
1 egg, slightly beaten
1 tsp. vanilla
1 c. pumpkin (canned)
1 c. chocolate chips or raisins
Halloween candies (optional)

Preheat oven to 350 degrees. Combine flour, oats, baking soda, cinnamon, and salt. Cream butter; gradually add sugars, beating until light and fluffy. Add egg and vanilla; mix well. Alternate additions of dry ingredients and pumpkin, mixing well after each addition. Stir in morsels.
For each cookie, drop a large spoonful onto a cookie sheet. Smooth into a pumpkin shape. Add a stem. Bake for 20 to 25 minutes until cookies are firm and lightly browned. Remove from cookie sheets. Cool. Decorate with candies, raisins or nuts, using icing or peanut butter if you want. Makes 2 to 3 dozen large cookies.

Carrie Duwe

Candy Cane Cookies

1 c. powdered sugar
1 1/2 c. margarine or butter, softened
1/2 c. shortening
1 egg
1 1/2 tsp. almond extract
1 tsp. vanilla
2 1/2 c. flour
1 tsp. salt
1/2 tsp. red food coloring
1/2 c. peppermint candy, crushed
1/2 c. granulated sugar

Heat oven to 375 degrees. Mix powdered sugar, butter, shortening, egg, and flavorings. Stir in flour and salt. Divide dough into halves and tint one half with food coloring. Make 4 inch ropes or "snakes" out of each color and tightly twist together to form candy cane. Curve the top over. 
Place on ungreased cookie sheet and bake for 9 minutes. Take out of oven and immediately sprinkle with peppermint crushed candy and granulated sugar mixture over hot cookies.

Fudge

3 c. sugar
3/4 c. margarine
3/4 c. evaporated milk
1 (12 oz.) pkg chocolate (semi-sweet)
1 (7 oz.) jar marshmallow creme
1 c. chopped nuts
1 tsp. vanilla

Combine sugar, margarine and milk in heavy pan. Bring to full roiling boil, stirring constantly (or else you will get brown flakes!). Chocolate Angel Cake Dessert

2 pkgs (6 or 7 oz. each) chocolate chips
2 scant tbsp. sugar
3 eggs, separated
1 pt. whipping cream
1 small 7 inch angel food cake or 
1 medium sized loaf cake

Melt chocolate chips with sugar over hot water. Remove from heat, add beaten egg yolks, and cool 5 minutes. Stir in stiffly beaten egg whites and whipped cream. Break angel food into bite sized pieces, sprinkle a layer in the bottom of a buttered round or square cake tin, using about half the cake. Cover with a layer of the chocolate mixture. Dot this with the rest of the cake pieces and top with the remainder of the chocolate mixture. Chill over night in refrigerator. Cut in small pieces to serve. Serves about 12 to 14.

Alice Marie Fuller

Donna's Spaghetti Sauce

1 clove fresh garlic, minced
1/2 c. red cooking wine, preferably burgundy
1 t. oregano
1 small can tomato paste
1 small onion, chopped fine
1 large can whole tomatoes
1 bay leaf
1 t. basil
1 cat sliced mushrooms, drained
salt, pepper, sugar to taste

Brown ground beef until all redness is gone and strain off fat. Salt and pepper the beef. Add onions and garlic at saute for a moment. Ass whole tomatoes (chopped up in their juice), bay leaf, mushrooms, tomato paste, and simmer for 1/2 hour. 1/2 cup chopped pecans
Jelly

Cream shortening and sugar. Add egg yolk. Beat until light. Blend in flour. Roll dough into small balls about 1 inch in diameter. Dip in unbeaten egg whites, then in chopped pecans. Place on a greased cookie sheet. Make a depression in centers. Bake 5 minutes in slow 275 degree oven. Take out and press down centers again. Continue to bake in moderate oven at 350 degrees for 15 minutes. Cool slightly and fill centers with jelly. Makes 1/2 dozen.

Chicken Enchiladas

Flour tortillas
1 cooked and boned chicken, shredded
1/2 to 1 c. chopped green onion
1 can chopped green chilies
1 can enchilada sauce (green or red)
4 to 5 T. sour cream
Grated cheddar cheese
Salt, pepper, garlic to taste

In large skillet, combine shredded chicken, green onion, 1/2 can green chilies, 1/2 can enchilada sauce, sour cream, salt, pepper, and garlic. Heat. Warm tortillas either in a frying pan or the microwave. Spread about 2 tablespoons of meat mixture on the tortilla and top with grated cheddar cheese. Cover with foil and bake at 350 degrees for 1/2 hour. Great served with salsa, refried beans, chips and a green salsa.

Octoberfest

1 qt. sauerkraut
2 apples, diced
3 T. sugar
1 tsp. caraway seed
1/2 c. white wine or water
2 lbs. Polish sausage or bratwurst

Place sauerkraut in a strainer and hold it under cold running water. Wash and drain sauerkraut several times. Place sauerkraut in crockpot and stir it into the apples sugar, caraway seeds and wine. Cut sausage into serving-sized portions and partially bury them in sauerkraut. Cover and cook on low for 7 to 9 hours in crockpot. Serve with spicy mustard and horseradish sauce. Serves 4 to 6.

Peaches and Cream Cheesecake

3/4 c. flour
1 tsp. baking powder
1/2 tsp. salt
3 T. butter
1 egg
1/2 c. milk
8 oz. cream cheese
3 1/4 oz vanilla pudding (not instant)
1 lb can peaches, 2 T. of reserved juice
sugar
cinnamon

Combine and beat for 2 minutes the flour, baking powder, salt, butter, egg, milk and pudding. Pour into a 9 inch pie plate. Drain peaches; place fruit on batter. Beat for 2 minutes, cream cheese, sugar, and 2 tablespoons reserved peach liquid. Spoon over fruit to within 1 inch edge of batter. Sprinkle on top a mixture of 1 tablespoon sugar and 1/2 teaspoon cinnamon. Bake at 350 degrees for 30 to 35 minutes. Filling will appear soft but will set as it chills. Chill well.
